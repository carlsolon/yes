Famous Tourist Spots in Bukidnon

Bukidnon offers a wealth of captivating tourist spots, ranging from breathtaking natural wonders to rich cultural landmarks. These destinations provide opportunities for exploration, cultural immersion, and adventure, drawing travelers from around the globe. Not only do they contribute to the local economy, but they also promote the region's cultural heritage, leaving visitors with unforgettable memories of their Bukidnon experience.

Dahilayan Adventure Park

If you're looking for places to visit in Bukidnon, look no further than DahiDahilayan Adventure Park. Located in Manolo Fortich, it's one of the best places to visit for a family vacation. Here, you can experience thrilling activities that will get your adrenaline pumping, including Asia's longest dual zipline. You can also try the Dropzone, a heart-pounding freefall experience, or challenge yourself with the Skytower Base Jump, where you leap from an elevated platform when you book a DahiDahilayan Adventure Park package. Take a relaxing horseback ride or enjoy the beautiful scenery on the Forest Park train ride for a more laid-back adventure. There are also kid-friendly activities like the playground and mini-golf course.

Communal
Ranch

Communal Ranch, an Impasug-ong, Bukidnon tourist spot known as the "New Zealand of Bukidnon," is one of the romantic places in the Philippines. At the Communal Ranch, you can enjoy horseback riding through picturesque trails surrounded by breathtaking views of Paminahawa Ridge. Explore the ranch's beautiful grounds filled with colorful flowers and plants. Take a moment to relax by the serene pond and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere. The ranch also offers exciting outdoor activities such as fishing and hiking to a nearby waterfall. It is also a great place for camping in the Philippines, allowing you to enjoy the beauty of nature fully.

Mt. Dulang
Dulang

If you plan to go on a Philippine hiking tour, include Mt. Dulang Dulang in your itinerary. Located in the Kitanglad Mountain Range in Bukidnon, it is the second-highest mountain in the Philippines and offers a thrilling experience for hikers. As you climb, you'll be rewarded with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes, making it one of the best hikes in the Philippines. Standing at 2,941 meters above sea level, this mountain in Bukidnon holds great significance for the Talaandig tribe of Lantapan, who consider it a sacred place. The mountain range is the ancestral domain of the Bukidnon, Higaonon, and Talaandig indigenous people, adding cultural and historical value to the area.

Lake
Apo

With a depth of 26 meters, Lake Apo is a crater lake in the Philippines nestled in the heart of Valencia, Bukidnon. This natural wonder is formed within the crater of an extinct volcano, adding to its unique appeal. The name of the lake, "Apo," originates from the Bisaya term that means "elder" or "grandparent." At Lake Apo, you can try various activities within the lake's crystal-clear waters, such as kayaking, paddle boarding, and fishing. One of the popular experiences here is floating on a bamboo raft, offering a peaceful and serene way to glide across the lake while taking in the breathtaking surroundings.

Monastery of the Transfiguration

Monastery of the Transfiguration is a Malaybalay, Bukidnon tourist spot offering a peaceful spiritual reflection retreat. It sits atop a hill and features a unique pyramid-shaped design by National Artist for Architecture Leandro Locsin, inspired by the traditional Igorot house. One of the main highlights of the church is the miraculous black and gold Madonna and Child image from Egypt, where visitors can rub their handkerchiefs on it. Take some time for quiet contemplation in the chapel, surrounded by beautiful gardens and a tranquil lake. Additionally, you can appreciate the beauty of the church's architecture and historical artifacts.

mt-capistrano

With a height of 2,000 feet above sea level, Mt. Capistrano, a Malaybalay Bukidnon tourist spot, offers stunning panoramic views and is particularly famous for sunrise hikes. It was named after General Nicholas Capistrano, a brave revolutionary leader from Cagayan de Oro. It's a must-visit attraction featured in most Philippine tours in Bukidnon. When you hike this mountain in Bukidnon, prepare to be amazed by the breathtaking views of the rice fields and other stunning mountains, such as the Kitanglad, Kalatungan, and Piapayungan mountain ranges of Central Mindanao. In addition to its stunning rock formations and caves, this mountain is also a great place to witness the mesmerizing sea of clouds in Bukidnon.

Mt-kalatungan

Considered the fifth highest mountain in the Philippines, Mt. Kalatungan, located in the Kalatungan Mountain Range of Bukidnon, is a volcanic mountain that stands at 2,860 meters above sea level. With its challenging terrain, it has gained a reputation as one of the most difficult climbs in the Philippines. This mountain in Bukidnon got its name from the phrase "praying mantis eggs" in the local language. Experience the sacred rituals of the Talaandig tribe before your climb at this Philippine volcano worth seeing. Hike through various landscapes and forests, including a breathtaking summit ridge trail to the campsite. Traverse south to the town of Wao, then take the challenge to trek on the final leg known as the "Dead Nails" Trail.

Panimahawa Ridge

Located in Impasug-ong, Bukidnon, Panimahawa Bridge is a must-add to your itinerary for its stunning natural beauty. This ridge, which stands 870 meters above sea level, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers. Prepare to meet fellow adventurers, as Panimahawa Ridge is a favorite spot for camping and hiking. The trail, often featured in Philippine adventure tours, is open all year round, offering stunning views no matter the season. You can even spend the night camping and fully experience the province's beauty. The trail includes diverse terrain with slopes, paths, and open trails, including a flat road for vehicles.

Kaamulan Park

Make the most of your Bukidnon adventure at Kaamulan Park, a government-owned park and zoo in Malaybalay City. The name "Kaamulan" comes from the tribal word "amul," which means "gather" in their language. This park is a haven for nature lovers seeking activities such as jogging, walking, and meditation. The park provides a perfect setting to relax and reconnect with nature with well-maintained trails, picnic areas, and a swimming pool. Aside from being a sanctuary, it is also a zoo where you can encounter fascinating wild animals like tigers, lions, and even the rare Cassowary bird. Remember to check out the souvenir shops for unique local handicrafts.

Limunsudan Falls

Limunsudan Falls, located in Sitio Limunsudan, is a must-visit destination in Bukidnon. Standing at an impressive height of around 870 feet, it is the second-highest waterfall in the Philippines. Its two-tiered structure makes it even more captivating, adding to its appeal. This waterfall in Bukidnon got its name after the area's abundance of "sudan" trees. When you reach the waterfall, you will see a strong water flow as it cascades down in two stages. You can swim in the pool below or relax and enjoy the calm surroundings while taking in the panoramic view.

RotyPeaks Ridge Camp

If you plan to go camping in the Philippines, check out RotyPeaks Ridge Camp, a full-fledged campsite in Impasug-ong, Bukidnon. Here, you can disconnect from the digital world and surround yourself with like-minded individuals, accompanied by breathtaking mountain views and pine trees. The campsite offers cozy cabins, clean shower facilities, a view deck, swings, and fire pits for bonfires. You can even rent tents if you need to get your own. They also provide a water source and a cooking service where you can have delicious meals prepared. You can also explore the nearby trails for trekking and hiking.

Alalum Falls

Alalum Falls, situated in Sumilao, Bukidnon, is a single-tiered waterfall with a height of 148 feet. "Alalum" is believed to come from the Lumad-Binukid word "madalum," which means deep. This refers to the deep catch basin of the falls. The cold water of the falls comes from natural springs in the highland of the Kitanglad Mountain Range. You can relax by enjoying the scenery of this waterfall in the Philippines and taking photos during your trip. For the more adventurous souls, you can take a refreshing dip in the cool waters of Alalum Falls. You can also explore the nearby trails and go on a short hike.
